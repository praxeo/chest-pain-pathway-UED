This tool guides you through the Emergency Department Chest Pain Pathway. Click below to begin.

Step 1: Patient Arrival & Initial ECG

A patient presents to the ED with chest pain. An initial ECG is performed.

Step 2: STEMI Evaluation

Does the initial ECG meet STEMI criteria?

Outcome: STEMI Positive

Activate the STEMI protocol and follow the 2013 ACC/AHA STEMI Guidelines.

Step 3: Evaluate for Non-STEMI Ischemic Changes

Does the ECG show any of the following high-risk ischemic changes?

High-Risk Ischemic Changes:
  • Transient ST changes (≥0.5 mm, horizontal or downsloping) during symptoms.
  • Marked symmetrical precordial T-wave inversion (≥2 mm).
The following are NOT considered high-risk ischemic changes for this pathway:
  • Normal ECGs, non-specific findings, LVH, RBBB, old LBBB, or paced rhythm (not meeting Scarbossa criteria).

High-Risk Pathway: NSTE-ACS Evaluation

The ECG is concerning. Are the patient's symptoms also suggestive of a NSTE-ACS?

High-Risk Pathway: NSTE-ACS Workup

Initiate NSTE-ACS workup. Is the hs-cTn elevated on the initial or 2-hour repeat?

Clarification: Transition to Main Pathway

The patient's troponins are normal and/or chest pain has resolved. Although the initial ECG was concerning, the absence of myocardial injury (negative troponins) means they can now be risk-stratified using the main clinical decision pathway.

Outcome: Consider Other Diagnoses

Work up other causes of chest pain with ischemic ECG changes (PE, dissection, pericarditis, sepsis, etc.).

Outcome: High-Risk NSTE-ACS

Consult Cardiology fellow for recommendations, consideration of LHC/PCI, and subsequent admission.

Step 4: Main Clinical Decision Pathway

The patient has no STEMI and no other concerning ischemic ECG changes. They are a candidate for the main pathway using hs-cTn and the HEART Score.

Step 4a: Symptom Onset

Did symptoms start ≥ 3 hours before the initial hs-cTn was drawn?

Single-Draw Pathway

Is the initial hs-cTn < 3 ng/L?

A "Yes" meets Rapid Rule-Out criteria for this specific pathway. A "No" fails the single-draw rule-out, and the patient requires a 2-hour hs-cTn.

Step 4b: Evaluate initial and delta troponin (2 hour) to determine next step

Which criteria do the hs-cTn results meet?

Step 4c: HEART Score Calculator

Calculate the patient's HEART score to confirm disposition for the Gray Zone or Myocardial Injury pathways.

Total Score: 0

Intermediate Risk Pathway

The patient has an Intermediate Risk HEART score (4-6). Engage in Shared Decision Making about the next steps.

Outcome: Low Risk / Discharge

Patient meets Rapid Rule-Out criteria. Discharge home with Primary Care (PDC) follow-up within 14 days.

Final Discharge Considerations:
  • ED Clinical judgment
  • Is chest pain improving or resolved?
  • Is a repeat 2 hour ECG unchanged (no dynamic ECG changes)?
Documentation Reminder: Per protocol, a HEART score should still be documented for all chest pain patients, even those discharged via Rapid Rule-Out.

Outcome: High Risk

Patient has a High Risk HEART score (≥7). Consult Cardiology fellow.

Step 5: Observation vs. Admission

Is an ED Observation unit available?

Action: Place in ED Observation

The patient is placed in the observation unit. Has the patient had prior normal cardiac testing?

(Normal LHC or CT Coronary <2 yrs, or normal stress test <1 yr)

Outcome: Admit to Medicine

Since an observation unit is not available, the patient is admitted to the hospital medicine service.

Outcome: Discharge

Discharge home with PDC follow-up within 7 days.

Step 5: CTA Candidacy

Is the patient a candidate for a Coronary CTA?

(Criteria: No known CAD, <65 yrs, no contrast allergy, can stay for scan, etc.)

Action: Coronary CTA

Order Coronary CTA after Cardiology approval. What are the results?

Action: Consider Other Testing

Patient is not a CTA candidate. Consider non-invasive stress testing if the last test was >12 months ago.

Abnormal CTA Result

Formal Cardiology consult is required. Is further inpatient evaluation or intervention recommended by Cardiology?
